Team -

Shirish Karande

Shirish wanted to be a cricket scorer but landed up getting a Ph.D. instead. His interests can be broadly described as applications of maching learning and information sciences. He is currently involved in a couple of India centric human computation and augmented reality initiatives. Previously, he has contributed to the areas of signal/image processing, wireless networks and energy management. He got his Ph.D. from Michigan State University and has since worked at University of California- Santa Cruz, Philips Research and TRDDC. He hopes to someday get back to the world of academia to work on some combination of AI and computational biology.

Venkata Pingali

Venkata is a scientist at TRDDC. His primary background is in Internet protocols and architecture. His emerging technical interests include bigdata and human computation. Outside work he is mainly interested in the intersection of politics, economics, and history. He has spent several years volunteering for a global education non-profit, which made him acutely aware of the need for quality educational content in local languages, and motivated his participation in Bhagiratha.

Srinivasan Iyengar

Srinivasan is a researcher at Tata Research Development and Design Centre. His current areas of interest include machine learning and crowd sourcing. Earlier he was active in developing products and creating valuable IP in the data privacy space. When not caught in deadlines, he likes to develop theories with not-so concrete proofs on human behavior, luck and hard word. His hobbies include star gazing (He can name more than 100 constellations!), reading (only interesting stuff. No special preference for any genre) and spending time in coffee shops thinking on the next big idea :) He completed his Bachelor's degree in Technology (Computer Science) from College of Engineering, Pune.
